Phishing Awareness
Prepared By: Gowsalya.S
Date: 23.6.2026
Introduction
Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers try to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted person or organization. Phishing attacks are commonly carried out through emails, text messages, social media, or fake websites.
Objectives
Understand what phishing is.
Identify common phishing techniques.
Learn how to recognize phishing attempts.
Follow best practices to stay safe online.
Common Types of Phishing
1. Email Phishing
Fraudulent emails that appear to come from trusted organizations.
2. Spear Phishing
Targeted phishing attacks aimed at specific individuals or organizations.
3. Smishing
Phishing attacks conducted through SMS or text messages.
4. Vishing
Voice phishing attacks carried out through phone calls.
Signs of a Phishing Attempt
Suspicious sender email address.
Urgent or threatening language.
Requests for personal or financial information.
Spelling and grammar mistakes.
Unexpected attachments or links.
Offers that seem too good to be true.
Prevention Tips
Do not click suspicious links.
Verify the sender before responding.
Use strong and unique passwords.
Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
Keep software and antivirus programs updated.
Report suspicious emails to the IT department.
What to Do If You Suspect Phishing
Do not click any links or download attachments.
Report the message to the appropriate authority.
Delete the suspicious email or message.
Change passwords if credentials were entered.
Monitor accounts for unusual activity.
Benefits of Phishing Awareness
Protects personal information.
Prevents financial loss.
Reduces cybersecurity risks.
Improves overall online safety.
Conclusion
Phishing attacks are one of the most common cybersecurity threats. By staying alert, verifying communications, and following security best practices, individuals and organizations can protect themselves from phishing attempts and maintain a secure digital environment.
